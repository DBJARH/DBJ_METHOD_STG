# CMM – Capability Maturity Model

## Unveiling the Architecture Capability Framework

### Introduction

Organizations pursuing improved development processes and better architectural control benefit from frameworks rooted in Capability Maturity Models (CMM). The Architecture Capability Framework, embedded within The Open Group Architecture Framework (TOGAF), offers "a structured approach to gradually improve their Technology-related processes."

### What is CMM

The landscape encompasses various specialized models including CMMI, SA-CMM, SE-CMM, P-CMM, and the Enterprise Architecture Capabilities Maturity Model (ACMM).

### Components of the ACMM

The ACMM consists of three primary sections:

1. **Architecture Maturity Model**: Defines six maturity levels (0-5) across nine key architecture characteristics
2. **Architecture Characteristics at Different Maturity Levels**: Provides comprehensive organizational capability assessment
3. **ACMM Scorecard**: Monitoring and assessment tool for current maturity evaluation

### Levels and Characteristics

**Six Maturity Levels:**

- **M0** — None (no capability)
- **M1** — Initial (ad-hoc)
- **M2** — Under Development (processes being defined)
- **M3** — Defined (documented, standardized)
- **M4** — Managed (measured, controlled)
- **M5** — Optimising (continuous improvement)

**Nine TOGAF ACMM Characteristics:**

1. Architecture Process
2. Architecture Development
3. Business Linkage
4. Senior Management Involvement
5. Operating Unit Participation
6. Architecture Communication
7. IT Security
8. Architecture Governance
9. IT Investment and Acquisition Strategy

Each characteristic is assessed independently, providing "a multi-dimensional view rather than a single score."

---

## ACMM as Foundational capability

The Architecture Development Method (ADM) requires organization hosting it on ACMM maturity levels 3 and above. Effective ADM execution requires sufficient capability maturity across the nine dimensions — deliverable quality is "capability-gated" by CMM foundations.

### Organizational Capability Implications

ACMM assessment determines organizatin readiness:

- **M0-M1**: Enterprise Architecture becomes ineffective without foundational structures
- **M2**: Minimum viable governance emerging
- **M3+**: EA can function with documented standards and executive support

Critical structural requirements include:

- Executive-level EA reporting relationships
- Cross-unit participation models
- Architecture Review Board with enforcement authority
- Strategic planning integration

---

## Maturity Building Strategy

Rather than immediately delivering architecture artifacts, effective engagement focuses on capability development:

**Phased Approach:**

1. **Phase 1** (3-6 months): Organizational readiness — governance establishment, role definition, process frameworks
2. **Phase 2** (6-12 months): Pilot ADM cycle with limited scope
3. **Phase 3** (Ongoing): Scale and continuous optimization

This methodology provides "longer, stickier engagements" than transactional architecture delivery, embedding transformation within organizational structures.
